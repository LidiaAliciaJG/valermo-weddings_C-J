Things To Do

MUSEUMS AND MONUMENTS

Templo Mayor Museum

Seminario 8, Centro Histórico de la Cdad. de México, Centro, Cuauhtémoc, 06060 Ciudad de México, CDMX

Immerse yourself in the heart of ancient Tenochtitlan, the capital of the Aztec empire. Explore the ruins of this important ceremonial center, discover the vestiges of temples dedicated to their main deities, and learn about the fascinating worldview and rituals of this pre-Hispanic civilization. The adjacent museum complements the visit with valuable artifacts found at the site.

VIEW

Palacio de Bellas Artes

Av. Juarez S/N, Centro Histórico de la Cdad. de México, Centro, Cuauhtémoc, 06050, CDMX

Admire the majesty of this iconic building, an architectural jewel that combines Art Nouveau and Art Deco styles. Marvel at its impressive murals by renowned Mexican artists such as Diego Rivera, José Clemente Orozco, and David Alfaro Siqueiros. Additionally, enjoy opera, dance, theater, and music performances in its beautiful theater.

VIEW

Palacio Nacional

P.za de la Constitución S/N, Centro Histórico de la Cdad. de México, Centro, Cuauhtémoc, 06066, CDMX

Witness important moments in Mexican history in this palace, which houses impressive murals by Diego Rivera that narrate the country's history from pre-Hispanic times to the 20th century. Wander through its courtyards and halls, and learn about the political and social evolution of Mexico. Furthermore, it is the seat of the Federal Executive Power

VIEW

Castillo de Chapultepec

Bosque de Chapultepec I Secc, Miguel Hidalgo, 11580, CDMX

Explore this beautiful castle located on top of Chapultepec Hill, offering spectacular panoramic views of the city. It served as an imperial and presidential residence and today houses the National Museum of History, where you can learn more about the different periods of Mexican history through its vast collection. Its gardens and surroundings are ideal for a stroll.

VIEW

Museo Nacional de Antropología

Av. P.º de la Reforma s/n, Polanco, Bosque de Chapultepec I Secc, Miguel Hidalgo, 11560, CDMX

Embark on a journey through the cultural richness and diversity of the pre-Hispanic civilizations that flourished in Mexican territory. Admire unique archaeological pieces, such as the Stone of the Sun (Aztec calendar) and the Olmec colossal head, and learn about their customs, beliefs, and legacies. It is considered one of the most important anthropology museums in the world.

VIEW

Museo de Frida Kahlo - La Casa Azul

Londres 247, Del Carmen, Coyoacán, 04100, CDMX

Delve into the intimate and creative universe of the iconic Mexican painter Frida Kahlo. Visit the house where she was born and lived much of her life, see her personal spaces, objects, paintings, and clothing. Immerse yourself in her personal history, her relationship with Diego Rivera, and the influence of her life on her artistic work.
