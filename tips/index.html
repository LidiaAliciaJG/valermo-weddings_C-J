Gran Hotel Ciudad de México

This iconic 5-star hotel, located right on the Zócalo at Dowtown Mexico City, boasts a stunning lobby with a Tiffany stained-glass ceiling. It offers elegant rooms and suites, some with Zócalo views, and a rooftop restaurant. Its historical significance and prime location make it a top choice.

Travel Note

The best way to get around Mexico City is either walking or using Uber. We highly recommend downloading the Uber app. Uber in Mexico is cheap and the safest! We do not recommend taking the metro or local buses.

Traffic

Mexico City's traffic is one of the worst in the world, especially during rush hour with unexpected road closures. Please take this into consideration when planning your travel itinerary. We wouldn't want you to be late for a very important date.

Where should I visit before/after the wedding?

Mexico is an incredibly beautiful, diverse, and delicious country! Below I’ll list a few of our top suggestions:

Jalisco

This state, a short plane ride away from Mexico City, has several heavy hitters when it comes to cultural contributions. You have the city of Guadalajara, with excellent restaurants and bars, as well as the birthplace of birria and tortas ahogadas (big sandwiches, topped with a delicious spicy gravy). You can also check out the town of Tequila for a day trip to sample, you guessed it, tequila! If you go to the western part of the state, you’ll find the beautiful city of puerto Vallarta. A hub for luxury stays and LGBTQ parties, this is a sharp change of pace from CDMX. Finally, there are numerous beautiful quiet towns along the coast like the surf town of sayulita.

Oaxaca

Possibly the most delicious state, the city with the same name will be sure to delight you with items like all things mole (there’s 7!), breakfast tlayudas, chapulines, enmoladas, que silly, and mezcal! The charming colonial city comes to life at night, with locals and backpackers alike dancing the night away. If a beach vibe is what you are looking for, head to Puerto Escondido. Roughly 6 hour bus ride south (the buses here are 100x nicer than in the states), this town is often referred to as Tulum, but 20 years ago so check it out! There are also beautiful crafts such as wooden carvings, rugs, blouses, candles, gold and silver jewelry, natural dyes, woven items, and various colored clay pottery.

Baja California and California Sur

California Sur is known for its main city, Cabo, as well as excellent whale watching. Baja California is where you’ll find the Valley de Guadeloupe, Mexico’s Napa valley. Pristine luxury stays are coupled with top notch wines, dining, and hiking.

Guanajuato

While the city of the same name is a nice visit for a day or two, the real reason to come to this state is San Miguel De Allende. Known for decades as an artist colony and hub for expats, this city boasts more than galleries. The streets are lines with mid-18th century houses and buildings and cobblestone streets. Also a secondary wine region in Mexico, you can visit the hot springs in the morning and the vineyards in the afternoon. You can rent a car or take a 4-5 hour bus from Mexico City.

Puebla

Have you ever wondered where some of the most talented chefs in New York City come from? Well, if they are from Mexico, it is probably here! With dishes like Chile en nogada, mole poblano (there’s most famous mole with over 20 ingredients), high stacked cemitas, you are sure to fall in love with this charming colonial gem at the foot of a volcano less than 2 hours from the city - it also has the oldest public library in the americas! You can also find onyx crafts as well as the world’s largest pyramid, Cholula! They are also known for their Talavera pottery which can only be made here (like champagne) usually featuring a white and intricate blue design.

Quintana Roo

While best known for cities like Cancun, playa del Carmen, and tulum, I’d suggest Cozumel for incredible scuba diving and snorkeling as well as a more laid back and less touristy option (relatively). You can also travel to the northern tip of the state to Holbox, often shown on social media as an undiscovered oasis with pristine white sands and very few tourists. You can also venture a bit inwards and meet Mayan communities (they speak some Spanish, usually one person speaks fluently) and learn more about their culture, food, music, and traditions.
