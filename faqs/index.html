FAQS

When should I start making travel arrangements?

ASAP! Please start planning now before everything fills up and prices sky rocket. Mexico is open for travel.

Do I need visa?

You do not need a visa if you are a citizen of US, Canada, EU, or UK. Just bring your current passport.
When you go through immigration, all non-Mexican citizens receive a small piece of paper from the customs officer that you will need to keep with your passport. *You will need this paper to leave the country*
NOTE: for all international travel, you need to ensure that your passport is valid for a minimum of 6 months after your travel. Obtain or renew your passport here: https://www.usa.gov/passport

What is the currency in Mexico?

The Mexican peso (sign: $, code: MXN) is the currency. You’ll have the opportunity to withdraw cash from ATMs once you land. The exchange rate changes daily. However, the peso usually hovers around 1/20 of a dollar. A quick rule of thumb to convert pesos to dollars is move the decimal place to the left one position, then divide the remaining number in half. For example, $200 MXN pesos is roughly $10 USD.

Tip is generally not automatically added to your check, unless you are a large party. In a restaurant, 10 - 15 % tip is common. For taxis/Uber, it is not expected unless the driver helps with your luggage (10–20 pesos is fine).
